## OTMA's Core Delivery Logic (Mapped to Formal Methodology)

OTMA's 4-layer process structure (Nomeação Estratégica → Quebra em Etapas → Linguagem Híbrida → Foco em Implementação) maps directly to formal BPM and process design standards:

| OTMA Layer | Formal Equivalent | What It Does |
|------------|------------------|--------------|
| **Nomeação Estratégica** | Process naming / process model | Creates a shared language inside the client organization — names that map to responsibilities and outcomes |
| **Quebra em Etapas** | Process decomposition (L2→L4), subprocess design | Translates intent into executable steps; prevents the gap between "deciding to do something" and "doing it" |
| **Linguagem Híbrida** | Dual-purpose documentation (human + machine readable) | Enables both human execution and future AI/automation integration — a single artifact serves both |
| **Foco em Implementação** | Process deployment / execution phase (BPM lifecycle) | Bias toward running processes over modeling them; aligns with OTMA's core promise: "the system operating, not a manual" |

This structure is methodologically grounded in the BPM lifecycle (Design → Model → Execute) and SDCA logic (standardize first, run consistently, then improve).

## Methodology-to-Service Mapping Table

| Methodology / Technique | OTMA Service Tier | How OTMA Deploys It | What Client Sees |
|--------------------------|-------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **SIPOC** | All tiers — always first | Scopes the process being addressed; aligns stakeholders on inputs, outputs, and customer before detail work begins | A one-page view that confirms "we're solving the right problem" |
| **SDCA** | Diagnóstico Operacional | Identifies whether any standard exists; if not, creates one as the first deliverable | A documented, named process with defined steps and a named owner |
| **PDCA** | Implementação de Sistema | Installed as the client's ongoing management rhythm; not a one-time event but a recurring check | Monthly or weekly cadence where the team checks performance and makes a decision: hold or adjust |
| **Swimlane Diagram** | Diagnóstico Operacional | Primary mapping artifact; shows who does what and where handoffs break | A cross-functional process map they've never seen before that explains the friction they've always felt |
| **Process Hierarchy (L0–L3)** | Implementação de Sistema | Builds the client's complete operational architecture — names every process, assigns owners | A structured list of all their processes, leveled and owned — often the first time the client has seen their operation documented |
| **5S** | Diagnóstico Operacional (physical/service environments) | Creates visible, immediate improvement to build organizational confidence; entry point before complex process work | A reorganized workspace, shared drive, or information flow that works obviously better |
| **Kaizen logic** | Ongoing / Expansão | Embedded in the operational rhythm; employees surface improvement ideas through defined channels | A feedback mechanism that captures what's broken and funnels it into the process owner's review |
| **VSM** | Implementação de Sistema (for clients with flow-based operations) | Quantifies lead time and waste to build the business case for a specific improvement | A before/after timeline showing exactly how much time is wasted and where |
| **TOC (constraint identification)** | Diagnóstico Operacional | Used as the primary diagnostic lens: find the one constraint causing the most pain before prescribing anything | A clear answer to "what is the one thing most slowing you down" — before OTMA touches anything |
| **DMAIC (adapted)** | Implementação de Sistema (data-mature clients) | Used as a structured improvement framework when the client has data systems capable of supporting measurement | A defined improvement project with before/after data — not a statistical exercise but a structured problem-solving sequence |
| **ISO 9001 Process Approach** | Implementação de Sistema (compliance clients) | OTMA's standard deliverables (process map, named owners, KPIs, documented procedures) satisfy Clause 4.4 requirements | A QMS-ready process architecture — auditor-ready without requiring additional documentation work |
| **BPM Lifecycle** | Ongoing / Expansão | The complete lifecycle (Design → Model → Execute → Monitor → Optimize) becomes the client's permanent operational cadence | A management system that improves over time by itself — not dependent on OTMA being present |
| **BPMN** | Implementação de Sistema (automation / IT clients) | Used when the process will be automated or integrated with enterprise software | A formal process specification that IT teams can implement directly |
| **Process Mining** | Implementação de Sistema (clients with ERP/CRM data) | Event log analysis of existing systems to produce evidence-based process diagnosis — replaces interview-only methods | A map of what actually happened in their processes, not what people believe happened |

## OTMA's Process Delivery Framework (Detailed)

### Phase 1 — Diagnóstico Operacional

**Formal methodology running underneath:**
- SIPOC to scope the target process
- Swimlane mapping of current state (as-is)
- TOC constraint identification: what single thing is most limiting throughput?
- SDCA assessment: does a functioning standard exist?

**What gets delivered:**
- Named process with defined inputs/outputs/customers
- Cross-functional map showing accountability and handoff points
- One identified constraint (the place to start)
- If no standard exists: a first-version documented standard

**Client's experience:** "They showed us something we'd been living inside of for years, in a way we'd never been able to see."

---

### Phase 2 — Implementação de Sistema

**Formal methodology running underneath:**
- SDCA to install standards for each process in scope
- Process hierarchy (L2–L3) to architect the full operational system
- VSM or swimlane for each process area
- PDCA as the cadence mechanism (usually weekly or monthly review)
- KPI definition per process (efficiency, effectiveness, cycle time, compliance)
- Process owner assignment for every named process

**What gets delivered:**
- Complete process architecture with named processes (L1–L3 typically)
- Documented procedures for each process (procedures, not just maps)
- Named process owners with defined accountability
- A performance review cadence with process-level KPIs
- Handoff from OTMA execution to client operation

**Client's experience:** "We now have a system that operates — not a deck about how to build one."

---

### Phase 3 — Ongoing / Expansão

**Formal methodology running underneath:**
- PDCA as continuous improvement engine
- Kaizen logic embedded in team review cadences
- BPM lifecycle (Monitor → Optimize → Design updated processes)
- New process areas added using the same L2/L3 architecture
- Process mining consideration as data matures

**What gets delivered:**
- Process performance reviews
- Updated process documentation as standards improve
- New processes designed and installed as the operation expands
- Increasing automation integration as the "linguagem híbrida" pays off

## How to Reference Methodology in Client Proposals

OTMA's brand rule is clear: **authority comes from delivering, not explaining.** Methodology references should create confidence, not curriculum.

### Recommended Reference Style

**In proposals and positioning:**
> "We use a structured diagnostic framework to identify exactly where execution is breaking down — typically a single constraint that cascades into multiple problems — before we design anything."

*(References TOC constraint identification + SIPOC scoping without naming them.)*

> "Everything we deliver follows an install-first logic: we standardize current state before we improve, because improvement without stabilization doesn't hold."

*(References SDCA→PDCA sequence without the acronyms.)*

> "The processes we document are structured so a person can follow them and an AI system can interpret them — the same artifact works for today's team and tomorrow's automation."

*(References linguagem híbrida + process documentation standards without academic language.)*

### When to Name Methodologies Explicitly
- In proposals to larger companies with formal procurement processes — credibility through recognized vocabulary
- In ISO 9001 or compliance contexts — the client's auditor speaks BPMN, process owners, Clause 4.4
- In conversations with technical/operations buyers who ask about methodology — answer precisely, briefly, without over-explaining

### When to Avoid Naming Methodologies
- In website copy and public content (methodology names signal "consulting that teaches")
- In initial calls with SME owners who want results, not frameworks
- Whenever naming it would prompt the client to Google it and try to do it themselves

## Authority Content: Post and Article Ideas

These are content angles OTMA can own that demonstrate process management mastery without teaching clients to replicate OTMA's work.

### 1. "O problema não está onde você está procurando" (Constraint identification)
**Angle:** Every client we diagnose has one thing creating the majority of their operational friction. It's almost never where they think it is. They fix symptoms while the root cause compounds.
**Methodology underneath:** TOC constraint identification, root cause analysis
**Why it demonstrates mastery:** Describes a sophisticated diagnostic lens in terms the client recognizes from their own experience. Makes OTMA's diagnostic value tangible.

### 2. "Por que a última melhoria não durou" (SDCA — the missing stabilization step)
**Angle:** Most operational improvements fail within 90 days. Not because the improvement was wrong, but because there was no standard to hold it. We fix this first.
**Methodology underneath:** SDCA before PDCA; the standardization prerequisite
**Why it demonstrates mastery:** Identifies a specific, recognizable failure mode. Positions OTMA's stabilization-first sequence as the explanation for why previous initiatives failed.

### 3. "O que os seus dados já sabem sobre a sua operação" (Process mining teaser)
**Angle:** Your ERP, CRM, and management software have been recording every action in every process for years. Most companies never look at it. We do.
**Methodology underneath:** Process mining, event log analysis, conformance checking
**Why it demonstrates mastery:** Makes a PhD-level capability legible without explaining how to do it. Creates differentiation from interview-only consultants. Raises the bar for what "process diagnosis" means.

### 4. "A diferença entre um organograma e um mapa de processo" (Process vs. functional thinking)
**Angle:** Your org chart shows who reports to whom. It says nothing about how work actually moves through your company. The friction you feel every day lives in the space between the boxes — not inside them.
**Methodology underneath:** Process thinking vs. functional thinking, swimlane mapping, handoff analysis
**Why it demonstrates mastery:** Articulates a fundamental shift in lens that most operators have never named. Makes the swimlane map feel like a revelation rather than a deliverable.

### 5. "O que significa ter um processo dono" (Process ownership)
**Angle:** Accountability in most companies dies at the handoff point. Nobody is responsible for the end-to-end result — only for their part. A process owner fixes this. It's not a job title, it's a structural decision.
**Methodology underneath:** Process ownership (ISO 9001 Clause 4.4, BPM governance), process thinking
**Why it demonstrates mastery:** Introduces a specific, actionable concept that most Brazilian SMEs don't have — and explains why not having it is costing them. Positions OTMA as the installer of this structure.
